<details> 
<summary>üö® Documentation & Persistence Protocol</summary>


#### To prevent scammers ...

... from hiding their tracks by deleting repositories or editing deceptive claims after being exposed, Wall of Shames follows a **strict Persistence Protocol:**

### Evidence Preservation:
- üì∏ **Immutable Forks:** We create immediate forks of every identified scam repository as permanent "Snapshots of Deception"
- üìã **Log Archiving:** All admissions of guilt, deceptive marketing, and technical failures are archived as screenshots and Markdown logs
- üîí **Archive Security:** Evidence stored in Wayback Machine + local encrypted backups
- ‚öñÔ∏è **Legal Documentation:** Chain of custody maintained for potential law enforcement use

### The "Wall of Shame" Mark:
> "You can delete your repo, but you **cannot** delete the record of your actions."

Even if a repository is deleted by the owner or GitHub, the evidence remains accessible on our platform to:
- Inform the community
- Assist law enforcement
- Prevent repeat offenses
- Protect future victims
